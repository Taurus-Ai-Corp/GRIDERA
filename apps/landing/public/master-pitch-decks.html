HARD QUESTIONS
OBJECTION 01

"Why can't Ethereum just soft-fork to use PQC signatures?"

Signature Bloat. ML-DSA-65 signatures are ~2.4 KB, compared to 64 bytes for ECDSA.

Injecting 30x larger signatures into existing EVM state destroys transaction throughput (TPS) and triggers catastrophic chain bloat. Legacy chains require complete architectural rebuilds, not soft forks.

GRIDERA bypasses this by offloading signature verification to our local engine and dropping compact, deterministic roots into the Hedera HCS.

"Isn't the quantum threat 10 years away?"

More importantly, compliance deadlines are immediate. NIST released FIPS 203, 204, and 205 in 2024. Enterprise and banking institutions are legally mandated to begin migrating infrastructure today to meet 2030 absolute cutoff dates.
